# synthesize-role-proxy

Use when the target role is unposted, confidential, internal, acquisition-related, referral-driven, or otherwise under-specified, but similar public postings or team pages are available.

## Inputs

- target company or team
- reason the role is a proxy target
- similar postings, team pages, career pages, or public role descriptions
- private career graph
- current skill inventory and do-not-claim guardrails

## Steps

1. Collect and cite each proxy source.
2. Cluster the sources by role family, domain, skills, responsibilities, seniority, and recurring language.
3. Separate common signals from one-off requirements.
4. Build a proxy role profile with:
   - likely role title family
   - recurring technical skills
   - recurring domain/workflow signals
   - recurring delivery practices
   - likely dealbreakers or risk areas
5. Compare the proxy profile against the private graph and skill inventory.
6. Draft a first-pass resume using only confirmed claims and careful adjacent framing.
7. Create skill-gap questions for source requirements that are not already confirmed.
8. Re-draft only after the user rates the gaps.

## Rules

- Do not overfit the resume to a single proxy posting when the target role is unknown.
- Do not claim vendor tools, regulated-domain experience, clearances, certifications, or platform-specific systems unless the graph supports them.
- Use proxy postings to infer direction, not to invent evidence.
- Keep the output clear that it is a first-pass proxy resume until the actual role is confirmed.
- Save proxy-source notes and skill-gap questions in the private workspace.
